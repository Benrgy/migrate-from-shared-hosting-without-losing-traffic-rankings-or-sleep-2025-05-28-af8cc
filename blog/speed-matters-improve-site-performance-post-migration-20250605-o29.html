Speed Matters: Improve Site Performance Post Migration

Published on May 28, 2024 • 10 min read

So, you've finally decided to migrate from shared hosting to improve speed. Smart move. Shared hosting is like living in a dorm—cheap, crowded, and someone's always hogging the bandwidth with their 4K cat videos. But now that you've upgraded, your site should be flying, right? Well, not always.

Why Speed Still Matters After Migration

Even after you migrate from shared hosting to improve speed, there's no guarantee your site will suddenly become the Usain Bolt of the internet. Shared hosting often masks underlying issues—bloated code, unoptimized images, plugins that haven't been updated since the Obama administration.

Common Speed Killers After Migration

1. Unoptimized Images

You wouldn't load a moving truck with boulders when you could just pack clothes. Yet, that's exactly what happens when you upload full-resolution images straight from your DSLR. Tools like TinyPNG or ShortPixel can compress images without making them look like they were taken on a flip phone.

2. Bloated Plugins & Themes

Some WordPress plugins are like that one friend who insists on bringing their entire entourage to every party. They slow everything down. Audit your plugins—if you haven't used one in the last six months, ditch it.

3. Caching Issues

Caching is like meal prepping for your website—it saves time by serving pre-made versions of your pages instead of rebuilding them every time. If you haven't set up caching, you're missing out on easy speed gains.

How to Actually Speed Up Your Site Post-Migration

  • Optimize Your Database
  • Enable Gzip Compression
  • Minify CSS, JavaScript, and HTML
  • Lazy Load Images & Videos
  • Upgrade Your Hosting Plan (if needed)
  • Monitor & Test Relentlessly

Real-World Example: A Slow Blog's Redemption

A food blog migrated to cloud hosting but still had 5MB images, 20+ plugins, and zero caching. After optimizing images, deleting unused plugins, and setting up Cloudflare, their load time dropped from 8 seconds to under 2. Traffic increased 40% in one month.

Final Thoughts

Migrating from shared hosting is a great start, but it's not a magic bullet. Speed optimization is an ongoing process—like keeping your car tuned up instead of just buying a faster one. Audit, optimize, test, repeat. Your visitors (and Google) will thank you.
